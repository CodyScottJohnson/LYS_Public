Senahu Guatemala

A more beautiful place cannot be found on earth! Located in the beautiful mountains of Central America, Senahu is a rare treasure of untouched beauty. From its tropical landscape to the gorgeous mountain views, you will see banana trees, swing from hanging vines and hear coconuts fall from the trees. It is only accessible by an extremely rigorous 12 hour ride through the mountains on winding roads, or by a 50 minute helicopter ride over the lush mountains! We choose the helicopter! It is very secluded, and you will rarely see another tourist. Second only to the natural beauty of Senahu is the beauty of its people. Although very poor, the people of the Senahu region are warm and welcoming. The majority of the population, being indigenous Mayan, have a rich and colorful heritage. Most live without electricity or indoor plumbing, and most have dirt floor homes, yet they are resourceful with what they have. While walking through the streets you will see the people wearing their colorful traditional clothing, hear the singing from distant churches and sounds of laughter, and smell fresh tortillas being cooked by local vendors.

Explore the deep, hidden caves of Seamay, Guatemala. This amazing cavern is known only to some local villagers. As you enter the cave you descend the antique Mayan carved staircase into a series of chambers and tunnels. As you continue exploring, you will find ancient formations, small pools and some rare wildlife. Hidden amongst the lush tropical landscape, the Se’amay caves will be an adventure you won’t soon forget!
